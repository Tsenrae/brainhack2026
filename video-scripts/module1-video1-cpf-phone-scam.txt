SCENE DESCRIPTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Setting: A modern Singapore HDB living room, mid-afternoon.
The protagonist — a young Chinese Singaporean man in his late 20s, dressed
casually in a polo shirt — is sitting on a sofa, scrolling on his phone.

The phone rings. Unknown number: "+65 6xxx xxxx"
He answers, looking mildly curious.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FULL DIALOGUE SCRIPT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[PHONE — MALE CALLER, authoritative voice, slight formal accent]
CALLER: "Good afternoon. Am I speaking with [pause] Mr Lim Wei Jian?"

PROTAGONIST: "Uh… yes, who's this?"

CALLER: "This is Officer Tan from the CPF Board Operations Centre, Badge
Number 4271. Mr Lim, we have detected suspicious transactions on your CPF
account totalling forty-seven thousand, five hundred dollars. These were
flagged as potentially linked to a money laundering syndicate."

PROTAGONIST: [sitting up straight, expression shifts to alarm]
"Wait, what? That doesn't sound right…"

CALLER: "Mr Lim, I understand this is alarming. For your protection, we are
initiating an immediate account freeze procedure. To complete this, I will
need to verify your identity — please confirm your NRIC number and your
SingPass login credentials."

PROTAGONIST: [hesitating, voice lowers]
"My… my SingPass password? Is this really CPF calling?"

CALLER: "Sir, this is a time-sensitive matter. Every minute your account
remains active, the fraudulent transactions continue. If you do not cooperate
now, CPF will not be liable for any further losses. Do you want to protect
your retirement funds?"

PROTAGONIST: [visible anxiety, fingers hovering over phone]

[FREEZE FRAME — on-screen text appears:]

  "Would you give your SingPass password to this caller?"

[End of video]

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RED FLAGS TO HIGHLIGHT (for quiz feedback screen)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Request for SingPass password over the phone — CPF NEVER asks for this
2. Unverifiable badge number — anyone can claim a badge number
3. Threat of liability if you don't comply — real agencies don't threaten victims
4. Time pressure to prevent verification — designed to stop you calling CPF directly
