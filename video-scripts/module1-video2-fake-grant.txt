Video Scenario #2: Fake Government Grant Announcement Video

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENE DESCRIPTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Setting: A professional-looking home office or studio.
A well-dressed woman in her 30s (blazer, neat bun) sits at a clean desk.
Behind her: the Singapore flag, slightly out of focus.
On the desk: a generic "government-style" plaque (blurred/placeholder text)
and a laptop.

The video mimics the look of an official government announcement video — but
has subtle details that mark it as fake (wrong URL lower-third, no official
watermark, stock-photo-quality background).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FULL DIALOGUE SCRIPT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[WOMAN speaks directly to camera, authoritative tone, slight urgency]

WOMAN: "Good afternoon. On behalf of the Ministry of Finance, I am pleased
to announce the Singapore Recovery Assistance Package — a new initiative
providing eligible Singaporeans with a one-time payment of one thousand, five
hundred dollars."

[On-screen graphic appears: "Singapore Recovery Assistance Package — $1,500"]

WOMAN: "To receive your payment, you must register before the deadline — this
offer closes in forty-eight hours, and slots are filling up quickly."

[Lower-third graphic appears: "sg-recovery-gov.xyz — Register Now"]

WOMAN: "Visit sg-recovery-gov.xyz to claim your payment. You will need your
SingPass credentials to verify your eligibility. Please act now — this
programme is available on a first-come, first-served basis."

[Upbeat music fades in slightly. Screen fades with:
"Limited slots remaining — act now before your payment is forfeited."]

[End of video]

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VISUAL DIRECTION NOTES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- The set looks official but has subtle tells:
    • The URL shown is ".xyz" not ".gov.sg"
    • The official-looking seal on the desk is blurred/not recognisable
    • No logo watermark in the corner (real government videos always have one)
    • The graphic font is slightly wrong compared to real government materials
- Camera: static, front-on, slightly too perfect — uncanny valley of "official"
- Lighting: studio lighting that is just slightly too bright/flat
- Include a small on-screen countdown timer graphic ("48:00:00") that ticks
  down, displayed in the lower-right corner during the second half
- Colour grade: clean and neutral, matching typical government video palettes

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RED FLAGS TO HIGHLIGHT (for quiz feedback screen)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. URL is sg-recovery-gov.xyz — not a .gov.sg domain (all Singapore govt sites
   use .gov.sg exclusively)
2. "Slots filling up fast" — government entitlements don't have limited slots
3. 48-hour deadline — government programmes are announced with long notice periods
4. Requests SingPass credentials — visit the link first, never provide credentials
   based on a video alone
