────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SCENE DESCRIPTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Setting: A casual home bedroom or living room. Spontaneous, "authentic" feel.
The protagonist — a young Singaporean man in his early-to-mid 20s, relaxed
clothing (t-shirt, maybe a cap) — films himself on a phone, selfie-style.

He is excited, energetic, speaks quickly. He keeps showing his phone screen
to the camera to display a trading app with a large profit figure.

This is the "friend testimonial" format — one of the most effective social
engineering vectors because it feels genuine, peer-to-peer, and not like
advertising.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FULL DIALOGUE SCRIPT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[PROTAGONIST — speaking directly into front camera, casual, excited energy]

PROTAGONIST: "Eh bro, I seriously have to share this. I've been in this
trading group for two weeks, and I already made over eight thousand dollars.
Look — look at this."

[He tilts the phone to show a trading app balance screen: "$8,247.50 — Total
Profit" highlighted in green. The app name is blurred/fictional.]

PROTAGONIST: "I know, I know — you're thinking this is fake. I thought the
same thing. But the guy running it, Sifu Kenny — fifteen years of experience,
he was a Goldman Sachs analyst — his signals are insane. Like, literally one
hundred percent win rate this month."

[He leans in closer to camera, lowering voice slightly as if sharing a secret]

PROTAGONIST: "I'm not sponsored. I'm not getting paid to say this. I'm only
sharing because the group only takes fifty new members a month, and there are
twelve spots left. The basic channel is free, but his premium signals — that's
where the real money is — it's only two hundred a month. And he guarantees
returns, or full refund. No risk at all."

[He shows a Telegram group screenshot — a chat with many members and a
"Sifu Kenny Signals" header. Message previews show: "BUY XAUUSD at 2301 —
TP 2340 — Hit!"]

PROTAGONIST: "Seriously — what's there to lose? Join the free channel first
lah. I'll drop the link in the bio."

[Screen fades. Lower-third: "Sifu Kenny Premium Signals — t.me/sifukenny"]

[End of video]

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VISUAL DIRECTION NOTES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Selfie-cam aesthetic: slightly shaky, portrait or very slight letterbox
- Environment: Singaporean bedroom — HDB wall, maybe a sports poster or anime
  figure visible in the background
- The trading app screen should look like a real app but with a fictional name
  and clearly positive (green) balance figures
- The Telegram screenshot should look authentic but use clearly fictional
  usernames and a fictional group name
- Lighting: natural light (window), not studio — enhances the "candid" feel
- Pacing: fast, energetic editing. Quick cuts when he shows the phone screen.
- End on a friendly, persuasive smile — the charm is part of the danger
- No background music until the final fade-out (then a subtle hook suggesting
  something is off — a single dissonant note)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RED FLAGS TO HIGHLIGHT (for quiz feedback screen)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. "100% win rate" / "guaranteed returns" — impossible in financial markets;
   making such claims for investment services is illegal under MAS regulations
2. "Sifu Kenny — Goldman Sachs analyst" — unverifiable claim; real professionals
   do not cold-recruit through Telegram
3. "Only 12 spots left" — false scarcity to bypass due diligence
4. Shows app profit balance — easily faked via screen editors or demo accounts
5. MAS check: always verify at mas.gov.sg/investor-alert-list before investing
